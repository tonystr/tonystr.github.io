Inverse Kinematics is the mathematical process of finding joint positions along an arm, from a given end-effector. This is commonly used in robotics along with forward kinematics, though its uses vary wildly. In video games, IK is commonly used for foot placement, snake-like creatures and ropes. This Extension may be somewhat primitive in comparison to how it's implemented in real-life robotics or AAA games, but it's fairly flexible, easy to use and well documented.

As of 2017, there are 27 functions included in the extension, no 3d support, and it's the only IK extension for GameMaker availible on the YoYoGames market.
